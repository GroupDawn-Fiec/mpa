What is Steam?

View All

Steam is a digital distribution platform developed by Valve Corporation, offering a comprehensive ecosystem for gamers. Launched in 2003, Steam has become a central hub for PC gaming, providing a vast library of video games, software, and other digital content. Users can purchase, download, and play games directly from the platform. One of Steam's key features is its multiplayer functionality, allowing gamers to connect, play together, and engage in online communities. The platform also supports digital rights management (DRM), ensuring secure and authorized access to purchased content.


Steam provides a range of services, including automatic updates for games, social features like friend lists and groups, and an integrated storefront showcasing a diverse selection of titles. The platform is renowned for its seasonal sales events, offering significant discounts on games and attracting a global audience.Additionally, Steam supports user-generated content through Steam Workshop, enabling players to create and share mods, custom items, and additional game content. The platform has had a significant impact on the gaming industry, shaping the way games are distributed and creating new opportunities for both developers and players.
